The avocado's name may have origins in the Nahuatl word for "testicles," but the only thing ballsy about our avocados is their taste!

The avocado (persea americana) may have originated in the Tehuacan Valley in what is now modern day Mexico. It had a wide range throughout all of Central and South America. It was first cultivated in what is now Peru, where evidence of cultivated plants have been dated as far back as 15000 years ago.

The avocado tree drows up to 66 feet tall, and the berry itself is 3-8 inches long and weighs 4-35 oz.

Today, 1.47 million tonnes of avocados are cultivated per year from Mexico alone.
